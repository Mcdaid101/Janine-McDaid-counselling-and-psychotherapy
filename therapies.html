Understanding Support

What is Counselling & Psychotherapy?

Counselling and psychotherapy offer a safe, confidential space to explore thoughts, feelings, and experiences that may feel difficult to carry alone.

It can help you make sense of what you are going through, gain new perspectives, and begin to understand yourself with greater compassion and clarity.

Many people come to therapy when they are facing anxiety, grief, trauma, relationship challenges, overwhelm, or a general sense that something in life feels out of balance.

As an integrative counsellor and psychotherapist I draw on different therapeutic methods including humanistic and person-centred psychodynamic and cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT). Sessions are tailored to your individual needs and pace.

Together, we create a space where you can feel heard, supported, and better able to move forward in a way that feels right for you.

How Counselling Can Help

Support for Different Life Experiences

Counselling

Counselling can help you talk things through in a safe, confidential space so you feel less alone with what you are carrying. It can support you in understanding your emotions, easing distress, and finding steadier ways to cope day to day.

Psychotherapy

Psychotherapy explores the deeper patterns, experiences, and beliefs that shape how you feel and respond to life. It can help you build insight, strengthen self-awareness, and create lasting change over time.

Grief & Loss

Grief can feel overwhelming, isolating, and unpredictable. Therapy offers space to process loss gently, honour what has changed, and begin finding a way forward at your own pace.

Trauma

Trauma can affect how safe you feel in yourself, in relationships, and in daily life. A supportive therapeutic space can help you process difficult experiences carefully and rebuild a greater sense of safety and control.

Anxiety

Anxiety can leave you feeling tense, restless, or stuck in constant worry. Therapy can help you understand what is triggering it, calm the nervous system, and develop practical tools to feel more grounded.

Lack of Direction

When life feels uncertain or you feel disconnected from yourself, therapy can help you reconnect with your values and priorities. It can give you space to reflect, gain clarity, and move forward with more confidence.
